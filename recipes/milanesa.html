Chicken Milanesa

Description

Chicken Milanesa is a classic Argentine dish consisting of thinly sliced chicken breast, breaded and fried until golden and crispy. This recipe offers a delicious twist on the traditional Italian veal Milanese, using tender chicken breasts instead. Served with a squeeze of lemon and your favorite side dishes, Chicken Milanesa makes for a satisfying and flavorful meal that the whole family will love.

Ingredients

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • Lemon wedges, for serving

Steps

  1. Place each chicken breast between two sheets of plastic wrap and pound them with a meat mallet or the flat side of a heavy pan until they are about 1/4 inch thick.
  2. Season the chicken breasts with salt and pepper.
  3. Set up a breading station with three shallow dishes: one with flour, one with beaten eggs, and one with a mixture of breadcrumbs and grated Parmesan cheese.
  4. Dredge each chicken breast in the flour, shaking off any excess.
  5. Dip the floured chicken breasts into the beaten eggs, allowing any excess to drip off.
  6. Coat the chicken breasts evenly in the breadcrumb and Parmesan mixture, pressing gently to adhere.
  7. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  8. Carefully add the breaded chicken breasts to the hot oil and fry for 3-4 minutes on each side, until golden brown and cooked through.
  9. Transfer the fried chicken breasts to a plate lined with paper towels to drain off any excess oil.
  10. Serve hot with lemon wedges on the side for squeezing over the chicken.
